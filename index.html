  • Bagni San Filippo

    Water depositing calcium carbonate for thousands years crafted this beautiful creation. In the woods of Bagni San Filippo people get together to enjoy a day, or a night, in these wonderful free thermal waters

  • Terme del Mulino

    The sulfur water in Saturnia gifted us with another creation. The several pools allow thousands of people every year to relax in these waters freely

  • Terme di Saturnia

    The pools of this Resort in Saturnia cover around 3,800 square meters, the clean water gets in the pools directly from the Earth, at a pace of 500 liters per second

  • ADLER Thermae Resort

    It is a luxury spa immersed in the rural Tuscany landscape. The spa offers a wide variety of treatments, including Yoga sessions and excursions in the nature around it, topping up its services with a rooftop restaurant

  • Fonteverde

    This Spa in San Casciano dei Bagni offers its treatments mainly indoor, from the Etrurian Salt cave to the saunas and massages. The Hydro-massage pools extend to the outdoor becoming a balcony on the green landscape

  • Grotta Giusti

    This unique Spa located in Monsummano has a large outdoor hot pool, and a Spa entirely located inside a calcium carbonate cave, with a divable natural pool

  • Le Caldane

    These thermal waters are located in Gracciano, they form a little lake. They have not a high temperature, and they are very pleasant to enjoy in the Summer

  • Mercure Spa

    The Mercure Hotel brand is present in 68 Countries but only in Monticciano they have a thermal pool. They have other 5 different pools both outside and inside.

  • Terme di Petriolo

    This thermal site in Monticciano is a bit hard to walk to but its worth it. The hot waters lay next to the cold waters of a little crystal clear river.

  • Terme Tettuccio

    This palace from 1779 in Montecatini has a modest offer in Thermal treatments, but the experience is linked to the High Society of that time and the atmosphere of their architecture.

  • Radicofani

    This little village is one of the oldest strategic locations. Situated in the Val d'Orcia park, its tower was first built in 973. Nowadays the tower that dominates the valley is a Museum

  • Pitigliano

    Pitigliano is one of the most iconic villages in South Tuscany, and in Italy. The 'Vie Cave' are ancient Etruscan roads digged in Tufo rocks that connect the village to Sovana, a trekking must.

  • Labirinto di Porsenna

    In the city of Chiusi there is a highly intricate underground labirinth reported by ancient historians as the mausoleum of Lars Porsenna, an Etrurscan king. It was instead an ancient system to provide clean water to the city.

  • Pienza

    This little village is declared World Heritage Site by the UNESCO. Its center was entirely rebuilt by the Renaissance architecht Bernardo Rossellini appointed by Pope Pius II to build an "Ideal Renaissance City".

  • San Gimignano

    Another UNESCO World Heritage Site. San Gimignano holds today 14 towers built by powerful noble families around the 13nth century, the architecture of the time preserved as it was with a few Romanesque and Gothic integrations.

  • Monteriggioni

    A Medieval walled town which, despite its reduced dimensione, faced several sieges but was never conquered in its all history. Its walls and towers are part of the city museum today

  • Piazza del Campo, Siena

    Siena's center is also a World Heritage Site. The city has been one of the wealthiest and powerful cities throughout Tuscany's history, its Dome and its square are just two of the beautiful things to visit in Siena

  • Piazza del Duomo, Pisa

    Piazza del Duomo in Pisa is world famous and doesn't need any additional presentation. Pisa has its own airport and this city might be the perfect start for a vacation in Tuscany.

  • Torre Guinigi, Lucca

    This tower is the last of the 250 towers of the 13nth century, is inside the walled center of the city. Lucca has been an independent State till 1799, fallen in Napoleon's hands. This city has a lot to offer.

  • Cattedrale di Firenze

    Cattedrale Santa Maria del Fiore in Florence is one of the city iconic symbols. As Pisa, Florence doesn't need any additional presentation. The city has also an airport to land in and start the trip in Tuscany
